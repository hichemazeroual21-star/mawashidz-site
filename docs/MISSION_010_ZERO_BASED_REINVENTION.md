# SECTION 4 — KILL THEM

| Company | Fatal Flaws | Survivability Rank |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| **A: WAKT** | Capago may block scraping; free Telegram competitors; appointment chaos may ease; users still need dossier + visa approval (#96) — alerts ≠ visa | **3rd** |
| **B: DAWRA Insurance** | Requires insurer license + capital; moral hazard; fraud on agency insolvency claims; rejection often discretionary — hard to parametrize (#33) | **5th (dies first)** |
| **C: HAK** | Air Algérie may ignore claims; 6–13 month culture (#13); success fee on $0 recovery; small ticket sizes; airline insolvency risk | **4th** |
| **D: DAWRAKT** | No clear willingness-to-pay for coordination alone (#65 shows price focus); WhatsApp groups already do this free; monetization weak | **2nd** |
| **E: MAKHRUT** | Cannot prove causation on rejection reduction; liability if user rejected after "pass"; consular opacity (#96); 45.8% rejection may be policy not paperwork | **1st** |

### Destroy in Detail

**A (WAKT):** **FACT** French Consul forbids intermediaries (#94). Monitoring borders on unauthorized access. When slots appear, 10,000 people rush — alert latency matters in seconds; push notification is commodity. **INFERENCE:** Race to bottom on pricing.

**B (DAWRA):** **FACT** 15% refusals from insurance non-conformance (#35) — insurable with right product. But **FACT** 21% financial proof (#34) and policy rejections are not independent events — adverse selection kills underwriting. **INFERENCE:** Only viable as rider to existing insurer, not startup.

**C (HAK):** **FACT** Tony Wheeler waited months (#9–10). **INFERENCE:** Success fee model needs volume; Air Algérie reputational resistance; passengers may not know rights (M007 ANAC Decree 16-175 awareness low).

**D (DAWRAKT):** **FACT** 82% family-motivated (#68) but **FACT** 56.7% book via agency (#61) — family coordinator still hands off to agent. **INFERENCE:** Tool becomes feature inside WhatsApp/Meta, not company.

**E (MAKHRUT):** Strongest evidence fit, but **FACT** rejection rate ~34% (#92) means most users still fail — product blamed. **FACT** document fraud penalties (#49) — wrong AI advice has legal exposure.
